# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, safe, respectful, and harassment-free environment for everyone participating in the Practice OS community.

Participants are expected to communicate professionally, respect different viewpoints, and focus discussions on improving the project.

## Expected behaviour

Examples of positive behaviour include:

- being respectful and constructive;
- accepting feedback professionally;
- focusing on the technical or product issue;
- helping new contributors;
- respecting privacy and confidentiality;
- avoiding the use of real client, patient, therapist, or production information.

## Unacceptable behaviour

Unacceptable behaviour includes:

- harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks;
- abusive, insulting, or deliberately disruptive communication;
- publishing another person's private information;
- sharing sensitive health, clinical, client, or production data;
- submitting malicious code or intentionally weakening security;
- repeated off-topic or bad-faith participation.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit, reject, or remove contributions, comments, issues, or other participation that violates this Code of Conduct.

Serious or repeated violations may result in temporary or permanent exclusion from the project.

## Reporting

Do not report sensitive conduct or security concerns publicly.

Use GitHub's private reporting features where available, or contact the repository owner through an appropriate private channel.

All reports will be reviewed as fairly and confidentially as reasonably possible.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to repository discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews, community spaces, and other interactions connected to Practice OS.
